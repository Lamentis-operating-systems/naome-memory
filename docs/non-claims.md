# Non-Claims and Safety Boundary

NAOME Memory is a research proof of concept. Its receipts make a deliberately narrow statement: declared inputs, a committed policy, explicit seeds, decisions, and resulting state are bound together and can be replayed. They do not establish semantic truth or human equivalence.

Version 0.1 does not claim:

- human-like memory, consciousness, cognition, or psychologically accurate flashbacks;
- that a semantic atom is true, complete, causal, or appropriate outside its source scope;
- that policy weights or thresholds are optimal;
- that random episodic retention is beneficial outside the frozen synthetic experiments;
- production safety for personal, confidential, regulated, or adversarial data;
- encryption at rest or in transit;
- security against a user or attacker who controls the local host;
- distributed storage, multi-host writers, network partitions, or cross-device synchronization;
- scalability beyond a measured cohort of 100,000 atoms within 30 minutes, 6 GiB RSS, and 10 GiB disk;
- generalization from calibration data to arbitrary workloads;
- compatibility with every future kernel version;
- a kernel authority mutation or an autonomous permission to retain data.

Only synthetic, non-sensitive inputs belong in this repository and its PoC databases. `retention_permission` is an application-level policy boundary, not a substitute for consent, deletion law, access control, or data governance.

An exact episodic atom is always labeled as one episode. Retrieval must not silently present it as a general rule. A digest proves identity and closure over declared material; it does not prove that omitted context was irrelevant.
